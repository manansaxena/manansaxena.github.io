Injury Prediction in Contact Sports and Rehab

Injury assessment during sporting collisions requires estimation of the associated kinematics. While marker-based solutions are widely accepted as providing accurate and reliable measurements, the setup times are lengthy and it is not always possible to outfit athletes with restrictive equipment in sporting situations. A new generation of markerless motion capture based on deep learning techniques is reaching maturity and holds significant promise for enabling measurement of movement in the wild. The aim of this project is to evaluate the performance of the state-of-the-art deep learning models such as Learable Triangulation, Videopose3D, Gast-Net etc for human pose estimation on a staged rugby tackle dataset

Human Activity Recognition

Recently, human activity recognition is drawing significant attraction with ever increasing usecases with major ones being healthcare monitoring systems and surveillance systems. However, majority of skeleton based models utilizes trivial temporal information of the skeleton joint coordinates and a few uses noisy upsampled images depicting the spatial patterns. In this project, we introduced a novel view-invariant human action recognition framework by using skeleton joints coordinates which exploit spatial shape structures of the skeletons and RGB Dynamic Images.

Classification of Breast Cancer Histology Images through Distilling Knowledge

Breast cancer is one of the most prevalent cancer types among women and recognizing it in early stages helps in it's treatment. Recent approaches to solving this utilizes heavy deep learning-based architectures, which take higher inference time and storage space for model parameters. In our work, we utilize a CNN architecture with a lesser number of parameters that can be effectively deployed on a resource-constrained device by developing two different approaches, namely the knowledge distillation and a hybrid technique combining attention transfer and knowledge distillation techniques.
